Factorial

n!n!n!

most basic use is to count the possible distinct sequences – the (repeatable) permutations – of nnn distinct objects

  • "there are n!n!n! different ways of arranging nnn distinct objects into a sequence."
  • ex. we have a square, a triangle and a circle and we want to know how many different combinations of order we can make ([triangle, circle, square], [circle, triangle, square] etc.)
    • Here, there are 3!3!3! combinations, or 6.

A factorial is a multiple of all values less than the factorial value

  • ex. 240 (5!5!5!) is evenly divisible by 5, 4, 3, 2 and 1
